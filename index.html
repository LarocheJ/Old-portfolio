Yournextbest

Adding value to kids going through school

The client I built this website for is a motivational speaker. He goes to junior and high schools and gives teenagers motivational talks. These talks are very impactful, however, he felt like he needed to do more. So, him and his team came up with an idea. The general idea was to shoot videos and upload them on a platform where those kids could go and watch when they needed to be inspired. They decided a website would be a great idea. Luckily, I know one of the guys on his team and he ran the idea through me. After spending a good chunk of a summer on the project, what you see here is what we came up with.

The website features 10 videos that a user can access after signing up and logging in. After each video, the user is presented with a few questions, which they must answer before being able to view the next video. All this information gets uploaded to a MySQL database. I created an admin interface and provided an access for the client and his team so that they can keep track of these answers and find ways to better help their audience.

It was a very fun project where I learned a lot in terms of creating a website from scratch as well as client interaction.

Grammy's Bakery

A mock e-commerce website

I created this website in school, at the end of the second semester. The goal was to create our very own e-commerce website. It didn’t need to be a full-fledged website, but the main components of an e-commerce website had to be present. We were given a logo and strict brand guidelines that were less than beautiful, which was intentional and part of the challenge.

We had to provide a way to filter at least 20 different items by category and sort the results alphabetically or by price. A user needed to be able to add items to their cart, see their total and check out. The order would then go into a MySQL database.

This was a fun project and I am happy with the final product. It was a challenge creating something from scratch that not only looked good with the given brand guidelines, but also provided good functionality.

First Time Muay Thai

E-learning project

This website was created in school. The goal was to create an e-learning platform. I was responsible for the majority of the backend side of the website development as well as some of the front-end and content for the modules while my classmates provided the media elements and some of the content.

The biggest challenge while developing this project was to come up with a way to create a clear system of progression where a user could log out, come back later and continue where they left off. We wanted to create a system where each user would have their own account. For that reason, we created a secure signup and login form.

New users would have their information entered into a database upon completion of the form and returning users would be authenticated by taking the entered password and comparing it with the encrypted one in our database. Then, a user could go on to continue or begin our e-learning course.

Space Adventure

Taking potential students through an NMPD Space Adventure

This was a group school project where students were tasked with coming up with an original concept designed to inform potential future students about the NMPD program. For this project, I was the frontend developer.

The team and I came up with a space concept where we take students on a space-themed adventure. Clicking on the rocket brings up a video that we made featuring an introduction to the program where each planet is a class and the planets were designed to resemble the teachers from each class.

It was a fun challenge to come up with a unique concept that would cover the entire program and keep it as simple as possible. We kept the design minimalistic and stuck to SAIT brand guidelines. Furthermore, we made the website responsive to accommodate the vast majority of people that browse the web on their phone nowadays.
